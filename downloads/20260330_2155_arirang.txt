Iran has dismissed a 15-point U.S. proposal aimed at halting the ongoing conflict, calling the demands "excessive, unrealistic, and unreasonable." 

Iran's Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmaeil Baghaei confirmed Monday that there had been no direct negotiations with the U.S., adding that all communications were limited to intermediaries.

Baghaei also addressed recent diplomatic efforts in the region, confirming that Iran has not participated in recent meetings organized by Pakistan, stating they were held within a framework Tehran has not agreed to.

 
2.
Seoul's gasoline prices have resumed their upward trend, hitting their highest level since mid-month.
As of Monday, the average gasoline price nationwide rose 0.7 percent from the previous day to 1,878 won,... or approximately 1.24 U.S. dollars, per liter.
In Seoul, prices climbed to the 1,920 won range, marking the highest level since March 11.

This upward trend appears to reflect the government's move to raise the price caps on petroleum products.
The government introduced a price cap system on refinery supply prices starting March 13, with plans to adjust the ceiling every two weeks.

In line with this, the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Resources announced the first adjustment of price caps on Friday.

With prices rising following the adjustment, analysts say the nationwide average gasoline price is likely to break the 2,000 won mark in the near term.

In the meantime,
South Korea's financial markets closed lower Monday, reeling one month into "Operation Epic Fury" in the Middle East.
The benchmark KOSPI ended the session at 5,277.30, dropping nearly 3 percent from the previous trading day.

The index has taken a severe hit since the conflict began.
Just before the operation launched on February 27th, the KOSPI closed at 6,244.13.
Monday's close represents a steep decline of roughly 15 percent over the past four weeks.

Foreign investors have been the primary drivers of this downward trend, dumping domestic shares amid a rapid flight from risk assets.
This flight to safety is also heavily impacting the South Korean currency.

The won closed Monday at 1,515.7 won against the U.S. dollar.
The exchange rate was already facing upward pressure earlier this year, closing at 1,485 won before the war started.
However, the onset of the conflict acted as a major catalyst, rapidly pushing the rate past the psychological defense line of 1,500 won.

President Lee Jae Myung has pledged o better protect the public from state violence.

Speaking at a town-hall meeting on Jejudo Island on this Monday... he said the government will abolish the statute of limitations for criminal and civil cases related to state violence including the April 3rd Massacre to hold those responsible to account.

The April 3rd Massacre saw the deaths of thousands of Jeju residents between 1947 and 1954 amid a brutal government crackdown on protests against a separate general election in the south amid the Cold War era.

BTS's new album "ARIRANG," ranked number one on the Billboard 200 for the week of April 4th.
The group now has its seventh number one album, a feat that began with "Love Yourself: Tear" in 2018 --- the first K-pop album to top the chart.

The latest album earned 641-thousand units, with the chart based on a combination of physical sales, digital downloads, and streaming.
It’s the biggest week for a group album since December 2014, when the chart began using units.

Over the weekend, BTS also topped the UK's Official Albums Chart with "ARIRANG" at number one, the third time in the group's career.
They first made history in 2019 with "Map of the Soul: Persona" becoming the first K-pop act to top the chart.
The lead track "SWIM" ranks second on the Official Singles Chart, leaving the group with a chance to climb to number one.
This already marks a new record, surpassing the group's previous peak of third place with "Butter" and "Dynamite," respectively.
The critic added that the latest album features several tracks that could earn recognition from prestigious awards such as the Grammys, noting that a win is possible if the songs remain on the charts through autumn.
