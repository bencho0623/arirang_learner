1. 
The Ministry of Foreign Affairs Thursday imposed a travel ban on all of Iran amid rising security concerns linked to the escalating conflict 
in the Middle East.
A Level 3 travel alert, which advises nationals to leave the country, 
was upgraded to a travel ban effective at 6 p.m., the ministry said, 
amid escalating tensions in the Middle East following 
U.S. and Israeli strikes on Iran.
The ministry warned that Korean nationals who visit or stay in Iran without authorization may face punishment under relevant laws, 
advising those planning to travel to the region to cancel their trips 
and urging those currently there to evacuate.

2.
Just a day after returning from his four-day trip to Southeast Asia, President Lee Jae Myung convened an extraordinary Cabinet meeting 
on Thursday to discuss the situation in the Middle East.
Lee described the safety of Korean nationals in affected regions 
as the government's most urgent priority.
He instructed officials to closely monitor the situation and prepare contingency plans for possible emergency evacuations, 
including the use of military aircraft and chartered flights if necessary.
The President also urged vigilance over potential economic fallout 
from the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, through which about a fifth of the world's oil is shipped.
He placed particular emphasis on energy supplies such as crude oil and gas, noting that disruptions would have direct repercussions across related industries and people's livelihoods.
President Lee says if handled properly, the situation could rather 
serve as a chance to strengthen the economy, 
from stabilizing overheated financial markets to accelerating the country's transition away from crude oil toward renewable energy.

3.
At the same cabinet meeting, 
President Lee Jae Myung also emphasized protecting the economy.
His remarks came specifically after the national average price of gasoline surpassed 1,800 won for the first time in about 
three years and seven months.
The national average price for regular gasoline rose 2.5 percent 
on Thursday from the previous day to 1,821 won per liter, 
while automotive diesel jumped 4.76 percent to 1,811 won per liter.
The president strongly emphasized the need to crack down on foul play, taking advantage of the current situation, and ordered the Cabinet 
to devise measures to prevent price gouging.

4.
The government issued Thursday a precautionary alert 
over a possible energy supply disruption amid an ongoing geopolitical crisis in the Middle East, vowing a stern response to unfair market practices involving energy supply and prices.
The government issued the Level 1, or blue, alert in the country's 
four-tier national resource security warning system to respond preemptively to a possible disruption in Korea's energy supply due to the Iran crisis, according to the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Resources.
The ministry said the country will raise its readiness 
against a potential prolonging of the Middle East conflict.

5.
BTS has unveiled a trailer for the upcoming concert special 
"BTS THE COMEBACK LIVE" on Netflix, heightening anticipation 
for the group’s long-awaited return.
The clip features flashbacks of past concerts and the cheers of fans 
known as ARMY, as the members share how much they have missed performing together on stage.
In the clip, leader RM says, "We promised our fans that we’d be back," reflecting the group’s commitment to return.
The live event will be streamed from Gwanghwamun Square 
on March 21 at 8 PM, and introduce tracks from the group’s fifth album, "Arirang," a 14-song album led off by the track "Swim."
