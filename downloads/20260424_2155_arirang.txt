1. 
President Lee Jae Myung wrapped up his state visit to Vietnam with a stop at the Imperial Citadel of Thang Long, one of Hanoi's most renowned landmarks. 

At the UNESCO World Heritage Site, Lee and first lady Kim Hea Kyung were welcomed by Vietnam's top leader, To Lam, and his spouse, Ngo Phuong Ly.

Lee wore a red-and-blue striped tie, while Kim appeared in a white jacket and skirt, a combination resembling the colors of South Korea's national flag, the Taegeukgi.
The attire was meant to express national pride while returning Vietnam's warm hospitality with equal courtesy.

The two couples toured the site before sharing tea outdoors while enjoying traditional Vietnamese performances.
On the same day, the Blue House also revealed the gifts that had been prepared.
For General Secretary To Lam, the gifts featured a traditional Korean painting of the mythical creature haetae and a pine tree, symbols of justice and integrity, along with a speaker bearing a caricature of the couple, reflecting his appreciation for classical music.

The first lady was given merchandise from the National Museum of Korea, a beauty device, and a traditional mother-of-pearl box.

Korea's tech-heavy KOSDAQ index soared above the one-thousand-200 mark for the first time in over 25 years.

According to the Korea Exchange on Friday, the index closed trade at 1-thousand-2-hundred-3-point-8, up 2-point-5 percent from its session on Thursday.

The significant milestone, last seen in August 2000, came as semiconductor equipment, materials stock, and biotech shares led broad gains across the index.

The rally was led by foreign investors who net-bought roughly 800 billion won worth of shares.
 
4.
As of Friday afternoon, the national average for diesel reached 2,000.53 won per liter, crossing the 2,000-won mark for the first time since July 2022. 

Gasoline is also up, averaging 2,006.45 won nationally and 2,043.94 won in the capital, Seoul.
To combat these rising costs, the government on Friday initiated its fourth petroleum price ceiling.

This keeps wholesale supply prices frozen at 1,934 won for gasoline and 1,923 won for diesel.
With this fourth review, wholesale caps have now been maintained for six consecutive weeks.

While the government cited prior drops in international benchmarks to justify freezing the wholesale ceiling rather than lowering it, global markets remain highly volatile.

In fact, on Thursday in the U.S.,.. Brent crude futures closed at 105.07 dollars per barrel, and U.S. West Texas Intermediate reached 95.85 dollars amid ongoing geopolitical escalations.

With international price fluctuations typically taking two to three weeks to reach local gas stations, the full impact of the recent crude surge has yet to be realized.
5.
The Blue House says there are no immediate concerns over Korea's oil supply for next month.

Presidential Chief of Staff Kang Hoon-sik told reporters on Friday that the country has secured 74-point-6 million barrels of crude oil for May, about 87 percent of last year's monthly average imports.

He added that Korea has reduced its reliance on Middle Eastern oil from 69 percent to 56... by bringing in additional supplies from the Americas and Africa.

Kang also said efforts are underway to diversify shipping routes, noting that nearly 24 million barrels from Saudi Arabia and 16 million from the UAE are set to arrive in May through routes that bypass the Strait of Hormuz.
