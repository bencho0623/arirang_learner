#1
President Lee Jae Myung attended the Korea-Philippines Business Forum in Manila today, where he called for deeper bilateral cooperation in manufacturing, energy, and infrastructure.
During his final day in Manila, President Lee took part in the Korea-Philippines Business Forum. 
The event brought together government officials and major business leaders from both countries.
They include the heads of South Korea's LOTTE Group and HD Hyundai, as well as the Philippines' SM Prime Holdings and Alliance Global.
In his congratulatory remarks, Lee highlighted the strong potential for the two countries to join hands in manufacturing by leveraging their respective strengths.
Wednesday's forum resulted in the signing of seven memorandums of understanding.
Among them are agreements to introduce new nuclear power plants and train skilled shipbuilding workers in the Philippines. 
The Blue House says the Korea-Philippines Business Forum marks the first business gathering of its kind since President Lee took office last June.
This underscores the Philippines' growing importance as a key economic partner for South Korea in the ASEAN region.

#2
On his final day in Manila, President Lee Jae Myung met Koreans living in the Philippines and vowed to make greater efforts to protect them from crimes.
President Lee also thanked the Philippine government and President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. for creating a "Korean Help Desk" at local police offices for the safety of Koreans.
He added that he has asked for help from the Philippine government to extradite a Korean national who is currently imprisoned in the Philippines to South Korea at an early date, adding that the person has reportedly been managing the export of drugs to Korea from behind bars via Telegram.

#3
President Lee Jae Myung will convene an extraordinary Cabinet Meeting on Thursday to discuss the situation in the Middle East.
According to the Blue House, the President will receive briefings from the finance and foreign affairs ministries and discuss the potential impact on South Korea, as well as possible response measures. 
Lee, who is currently on a four-day trip to Southeast Asia, has instructed the government to maintain an emergency response system led by Prime Minister Kim Min-seok.

#4
War fears in Iran sent shockwaves through South Korea’s stock market, leaving the country’s benchmark indexes with double-digit losses.
The KOSPI opened 3-point-4 percent lower and extended losses through the morning, eventually triggering a circuit breaker after the index stayed down more than 8 percent for over a minute, halting trading for 20 minutes.
The index closed at 5-thousand 93-point-5-4, plunging more than 12 percent from the previous session and falling back to the 5-thousand level.
The KOSDAQ also remained under pressure.
The index opened 2-point-2-5 percent lower than the previous day and extended its losses, triggering its first sell-side sidecar of the year.
The decline continued, eventually prompting a circuit breaker late in the morning, and sending the index below the 1-thousand level.
With Middle East tensions shaking markets across Asia, attention is turning to why South Korea’s stock market has been hit particularly hard.

#5
South Korea’s rival parties have agreed to fast-track a special bill aimed at facilitating large-scale investment in the United States, citing concerns that further delays could invite trade retaliation.
On Wednesday, the National Assembly special committee handling said legislation began formal negotiations on nine related bills, with both sides aiming to end the review process by March 9th. 
Lawmakers say a broad consensus has already been reached on major issues, including transparency, oversight, and whether to establish a separate investment entity.
The agreement comes following concerns that delays could trigger strong trade retaliation from Washington amid heightened global tensions, including conflict involving the U.S. and Iran.
