#2
Presidential chief of staff Kang Hoon-sik said on Friday that a passenger flight carrying South Korean nationals had left Dubai and was scheduled to arrive at Incheon International Airport this evening.
His remarks came during a briefing at Cheong Wa Dae on government support for the evacuation of Koreans from the United Arab Emirates.
He said commercial flights from Abu Dhabi are expected to resume on Saturday, while a chartered Korean Air flight will also be dispatched, noting that he held talks with Khaldoon Khalifa Al Mubarak, chairman of Abu Dhabi's Executive Affairs Authority.
Kang said 18,000 South Koreans are currently in 14 Middle East nations, 
with around 4,900 of them being short-term travelers. 
Of these, 3,500 are currently staying in the UAE and Qatar, 
waiting to return home.
The government has been stepping up efforts to support citizens in the region.
The foreign ministry said it will dispatch joint rapid response teams to Dubai and Muscat to assist Korean nationals.
Officials say the UAE currently hosts the largest number 
of short-term Korean residents in the Middle East, with about 3,000 staying there.
Foreign Minister Cho Hyun also held a phone call with UAE Foreign Minister Abdullah bin Zayed Al Nahyan, requesting the Emirati government’s support 
for the swift and safe return of Korean nationals, 
including the possible arrangement of charter flights.

#3 
South Korea will import six million barrels of crude oil 
from the United Arab Emirates as a contingency measure 
following the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, 
through which about 70 percent of its crude oil imports pass.
Presidential Chief of Staff Kang Hoon-sik said on Friday that 
two Korean oil tankers will be loaded with a combined four million barrels supplied by the Abu Dhabi National Oil Company.
The shipment will be loaded at a UAE port that does not require passage 
through the Strait of Hormuz. 
Kang added that the UAE has also pledged to provide 
another two million barrels from joint strategic reserves stored in South Korea. 

#4
The closure of the Hormuz Strait has caused oil and gas prices to skyrocket 
and driven global energy trade into turmoil.
Global oil prices have surged by roughly 16 percent in roughly a weeks' time, but U.S. President Donald Trump is unconcerned, 
as he stated during an interview with Reuters on Thursday that 
the current rise in gasoline prices globally
and its consequent impact on the U.S market is not as critical an issue
 compared to the necessity of military action against the Iranian regime. 

#5
South Korea’s consumer inflation held steady at 2-point-zero percent in February, according to data released by the Ministry of Data and Statistics on Friday.
That marks the sixth straight month in the two-percent range, 
in line with the Bank of Korea’s target.
Service prices rose 2-point-6 percent, driven by personal services
while core inflation indicators remained in the mid-2 percent range.
Yet the key factor behind the stable headline figure was petroleum prices, 
which fell 2-point-4 percent from a year earlier,
turning negative for the first time in six months.
